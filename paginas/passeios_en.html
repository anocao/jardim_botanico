↳ some tips of tours that you can do when you are in the Botanical Garden of Curitiba.

  • BOTANICAL MUSEUM

    • The project is by the architect Abrão Assad, who also planned the Botanical Museum, incorporated into the Botanical Garden in 1992, with auditorium, research center, space for specialized library and temporary and permanent exhibition room. Currently the Botanical Museum of Curitiba has the fourth largest herbarium in the country, with approximately 400 thousand exsiccatas – dried plants prepared for botanical collection – as well as a collection of samples of wood and fruits. And in the park there is a research center for the flora of Paraná and Brazil. The Botanical Museum of the Botanical Garden of Curitiba has an exhibition space, library and auditorium.

    Currently, the Botanical Museum of Curitiba has the fourth largest herbarium in the country, with about 400,000 dried plants and, of course, a large number of wood and fruit samples.

    But not only that, in addition to the incredible collections mentioned, the park also has a botanical research center in the state of Paraná and in Brazil. It is worth visiting and knowing more about the collections and research carried out by the Botanical Garden of Curitiba.

  • GREENHOUSE

    • The garden contains numerous plant specimens from Brazil and other countries, scattered throughout alleys and greenhouses of iron and glass, the main one with three vaults of the Art Nouveau style was inspired by the Crystal Palace of London, of the nineteenth century.

    The greenhouse is air-conditioned and maintains species from the Atlantic Forest such as Caraguatá, Caetê and Palmito. From its interior it is possible to have a privileged view of the garden in French style.

    The main conservatory features three Art Nouveau vaults inspired by the nineteenth-century London Crystal Palace.

    It is a super privilege to see the French garden from its interior. When we think of the Botanical Garden of Curitiba, the first thing that comes to mind is the main greenhouse.

  • GARDEN OF SENSATIONS

    • Inaugurated in December 2008, the Garden of Sensations is a space delimited by a living fence, where the feelings of the visitor are tempted, through direct contact with plants of different shapes, textures and aromas.

    Through the fence and the vegetable tunnel it is possible to see the colors of nature, feel with your hands the texture, shape and size of the plants, hear the sound of the waterfall and the wind, smell the flowers and vegetation. The route can be done blindfolded or not.

    The plants are also identified in braille and the monitors are available for the clarification of doubts and more detailed explanations.

    Are part of the scenario, still, beds and a waterfall, as well as an installation of the Honey Gardens program with child bees, without sting.

  • FRANS KRAJCBERG CULTURAL SPACE

    • The Frans Krajcberg Cultural Space, with the permanent exhibition "The Revolt", of works by the Polish naturalized Brazilian artist Frans Krajcberg. The name "The Revolt" expresses the feeling of the artist regarding the unlimited destruction caused by man in the Brazilian forests.

    In this gallery are exhibited 110 large works, all of them made from the remains of trees burned or felled illegally. There is also an exhibition of photos taken by the sculptor himself, sale of books related to the artist and the possibility of monitored visits. The main purpose of the space is, according to Krajcberg, environmental awareness.

    Vines, logs of burnt wood are the raw material of his creations, always in the sense of arousing indignation against the devastating actions of man.

    Among the works that make up the collection of the new space are sculptures, reliefs and photographs, as well as videos, texts and publications that will be the basis for future educational actions and will serve for the formation of a documentation center.
